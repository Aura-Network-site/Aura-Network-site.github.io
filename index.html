Proof-of-stake consensus

How to Stake AURA: Validator Selection and Delegation Guide

AURA staking follows the standard Cosmos SDK delegation model — token holders delegate to validators who produce blocks on their behalf, earning a share of staking rewards minus validator commission.

Staking step-by-step

  1. Set up a Cosmos wallet — Keplr or Leap wallet support Aura Network. Add Aura Network chain to your wallet via the official Aura documentation or Keplr's chain registry.
  2. Acquire AURA tokens — buy AURA on supported exchanges and transfer to your Cosmos wallet address (starts with "aura1...").
  3. Review validators on Aurascan — compare validators by commission rate, uptime, voting power, and governance participation at aurascan.io/validators.
  4. Delegate AURA — select your chosen validator and delegate your AURA. The delegation is recorded on-chain and rewards begin accruing immediately.
  5. Claim rewards manually — AURA staking rewards must be manually claimed (Cosmos SDK standard). Claim and optionally redelegate for compounding.
  6. Monitor validator health — if your validator is jailed or increases commission significantly, redelegate to a healthier validator. Redelegation is instant (with a cooldown before redelegating again from the same validator).

Best Practices for AURA Stakers and Aura Ecosystem Participants

For AURA stakers

  • Research validators on Aurascan before delegating — evaluate commission rate, uptime percentage, self-bond amount, and governance participation. A validator that never votes on governance is less aligned with network health.
  • Diversify across 2–3 validators for significant AURA positions — reduces slashing risk concentration and supports network decentralisation.
  • Account for the 21-day unbonding period before staking — AURA locked in staking cannot be sold for 21 days after undelegation is initiated. Use re-delegation (instant, with cooldown) to switch validators without unbonding.
  • Compound rewards regularly — claim and redelegate accumulated rewards to increase your staking principal. Gas cost on Aura is minimal, making frequent compounding economically viable.

For NFT creators and collectors

  • Verify NFT contracts on Aurascan before purchasing — check contract deployment date, creator wallet history, and whether source code is verified. Never buy NFTs from contracts with no verifiable creator identity.
  • Keep AURA for gas when bridging NFTs or tokens via IBC — IBC transfers require a small AURA gas fee on Aura's side. Always maintain a small AURA balance even if your primary holdings are in other assets.
  • Use official Aura Network resources for wallet setup — add the Aura chain to Keplr via the official docs.aura.network guide to ensure you're using the correct chain parameters and RPC endpoints.

Troubleshooting Aura Network: Staking Rewards, Wallet Setup, and IBC Transfers

"My AURA staking rewards aren't appearing"

  • Cosmos SDK rewards must be manually claimed — they don't auto-transfer to your wallet. Open Keplr or Leap, navigate to the Aura Network staking section, and click "Claim rewards" to collect your accumulated AURA.
  • Check your validator's status on Aurascan — if the validator is jailed, rewards are paused until unjailing. Redelegate to an active validator to resume earning.
  • Very small reward amounts may not be visible in the wallet UI until they exceed a display threshold. Check your exact pending reward on Aurascan by searching your address.

"I can't add Aura Network to Keplr"

  • Use the official Aura Network documentation (docs.aura.network) to add the chain — it provides the exact chain ID, RPC endpoint, and REST endpoint needed for Keplr's custom chain addition.
  • Alternatively, Aura Network may be available in Keplr's built-in chain list — search for "Aura" in Keplr's chain management settings before adding manually.

"My IBC transfer to/from Aura seems stuck"

  • Check the IBC transfer status on Mintscan using your transaction hash — Mintscan shows IBC packet relay status across chains and identifies if a relayer picked up the packet.
  • IBC transfers occasionally time out if no relayer processes them within the timeout window — if timed out, the tokens are returned to your source address. Check source chain for a refund.
  • If the transfer shows as "pending" for over 30 minutes, it may be waiting for a relayer. IBC transfers require active relayer infrastructure — most major channels are well-maintained, but newer channels may have less reliable relaying.
Aurascan is ground truth for Aura: For any on-chain activity verification, aurascan.io is the authoritative record. Wallet UIs may lag on-chain state — always cross-reference with Aurascan for transaction and staking status.
